QUESTION: What are the different types of government loans available for small businesses and farmers?
ANSWER: The Indian Government offers several major loan schemes depending on your needs:
1. PM MUDRA Yojana: For non-farm small businesses. Get up to ₹10 Lakh without collateral. Categories: Shishu (up to ₹50k), Kishore (up to ₹5L), and Tarun (up to ₹10L).
2. Kisan Credit Card (KCC): For farmers to buy seeds and equipment. Provides short-term loans up to ₹3 Lakh at low interest (around 4%).
3. PM SVANidhi: For street vendors to get a working capital loan of ₹10,000 to restart businesses.
4. Stand-Up India: For women and SC/ST entrepreneurs starting a manufacturing or trading business, offering loans between ₹10 Lakh and ₹1 Crore.

QUESTION: How do I apply for a PM MUDRA Loan?
ANSWER: You do not need an agent to apply for a MUDRA loan.
1. Prepare documents: Aadhaar, PAN card, and a simple business plan.
2. Visit the official portal (udyamimitra.in) or your local bank branch.
3. Fill out the application form. No collateral is required.
4. Submit your documents and wait for bank verification.

QUESTION: Who is eligible for a Kisan Credit Card?
ANSWER: Farmers, tenants, sharecroppers, and some allied activity workers such as dairy, poultry, and fisheries workers may be eligible.
1. You must have valid identification and land or activity proof.
2. The loan is used for seeds, fertilizers, pesticides, and farm needs.
3. Check with your nearest bank or cooperative society for exact eligibility.

QUESTION: What is PM SVANidhi?
ANSWER: PM SVANidhi is a government micro-loan scheme for street vendors.
1. It provides a small working capital loan to restart or grow vending businesses.
2. The first loan amount is ₹10,000.
3. Timely repayment can help you get bigger second and third loans.
4. You can apply through banks, microfinance institutions, or the official portal.

QUESTION: What is Stand-Up India?
ANSWER: Stand-Up India helps women and SC/ST entrepreneurs start new businesses.
1. It offers bank loans from ₹10 Lakh to ₹1 Crore.
2. It is meant for greenfield enterprises in manufacturing, services, or trading.
3. You must check the official eligibility conditions with your bank.
